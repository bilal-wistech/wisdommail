GLOBAL BRITAIN

BCC Trade Update

  • The BCC raised the issue of VAT challenges faced by UK exporters to the EU in formal dialogues with senior UK and EU officials at the Civil Society Forum meeting last Friday. Read the Joint Statement reached by business, labour and third sector stakeholders at the meetings in Brussels.
  • UK trade negotiations with the six Gulf Co-operation Council states resumed this week. Yesterday, the BCC met with officials from the Department for Business and Trade (DBT) to discuss our priorities for discussions on trade documents, services, business mobility and removing non-tariff barriers to trade in goods.
  • The UK Prime Minister is due to meet the European Commission President, Ursula von der Leyen, for discussions on UK-EU relations next week. Earlier this week, the BCC discussed recent policy developments in the UK and Brussels with the EU Mission in London.
  • The BCC joined the new DBT SME Policy Insights Forum to consider how trade deals can work to increase SME exports on their implementation.
  • InvestHK visited the BCC offices this week to highlight Hong Kong’s advantages as a strategic location for UK businesses looking to invest. The discussions also covered the development of the Northern Metropolis and broader opportunities within the Greater Bay Area for business expansion.
  • On Tuesday, the Trade Remedies Authority (TRA) policy team spoke with Chamber members at the BCC Trade Policy Committee meeting. As well as providing insight into how Chambers can influence decisions being made by the TRA on trade remedies, there was also a discussion on the tariff rate quotas recently increased for certain steel imports.
  • The TRA launched an enquiry this week into UK imports of tin mill products from China which are often used in packaging. This was following complaints from industry that these imports should face anti-dumping duties.
  • DBT is holding webinars during October on the new Product Regulation and Metrology Bill and what it means for goods markings and conformity assessment.
  • UK Export Finance is hosting a Supplier Fair on Tuesday 22 October for the Sokodé - Benin Border Road project in Togo, in partnership with Hitech Construction Africa. See further details here.
  • The next virtual meeting of the BCC Trade Policy Committee will take place on Tuesday 22 October at 14:00 (UK time). In addition to the usual agenda items, guest speakers will discuss trade and investment opportunities in the Gulf region. Contact William Bain for joining details.

Reforms to apprenticeship system in England

In his speech at the Labour Party Conference, the Prime Minister, Keir Starmer MP, mentioned that the government had published plans for reform of apprenticeships in England.

In response to the announcement, Alex Veitch, BCC Director of Policy, said:

"Skills shortages continue to be a major concern for businesses and a drag on economic growth."

"The proposed new Growth and Skills Levy was a key part of the Government’s plans at the election. It’s welcome ministers have acted early to give more details about skills reform."

“We’ve long argued that the current Apprenticeship Levy needs urgent reform to make it more flexible. Businesses need a simple, coherent and responsive system that properly incentivises employer investment in training."

Find out more about UK Export Finance

UK Export Finance (UKEF) is the UK government’s export credit agency. They help ensure that no viable UK export fails for a lack of finance or insurance.

  • Increase working capital: If a cash backed bond or guarantee is required to support an export contract, UKEF can provide a partial guarantee to your bank which will release the cash back to your business and ease the strain on your working capital.
  • On the ground support: Our regional Export Finance Managers can help guide you through the complexities of trade finance and credit insurance. They can help you get a better understanding of your specific export finance requirements and, where possible, identify an appropriate solution to support your export transactions.

Share with your members as relevant and contact UKEF for more information. You can also find out more about UKEF as part of the British Business Bank’s Finance Week.
